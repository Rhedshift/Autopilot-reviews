Most webcams are built for casual business meetings, but the **Elgato Facecam** is engineered for high-performance broadcasting. It eschews traditional webcam compromises by stripping out the microphone, ditching slow autofocus, and focusing 100% of its resources on delivering the cleanest, uncompressed **1080p video at 60 frames per second** possible. Powered by a studio-grade Sony STARVIS sensor and an extensive companion software app, the Facecam is the absolute best choice for Twitch streamers, YouTubers, and high-profile presenters who demand professional-grade visuals.

## Key Specifications
* **Max Resolution & Framerate:** Full HD 1080p at 60fps (Uncompressed)
* **Sensor Type:** Sony STARVIS CMOS Sensor
* **Lens Type:** Elgato Prime Lens (All-glass f/2.4 24mm equivalent)
* **Field of View (FOV):** 82° (Fixed)
* **Focus Type:** Fixed Focus (Optimal range: 30cm to 120cm)
* **Microphone:** None (Designed for use with dedicated external microphone)
* **Connection:** Detachable USB-C port on camera (includes high-speed USB 3.0 cable)
* **Privacy:** Includes physical snap-on lens cap
* **Onboard Memory:** Flash memory saves settings directly to camera hardware
* **Software:** Camera Hub (Windows and macOS)

## Who It’s For
The Elgato Facecam is designed specifically for gaming streamers, YouTubers, professional product demonstrators, and remote presenters who already have a dedicated microphone setup. If you need silky-smooth 60fps video to match your high-refresh-rate gameplay or demand meticulous manual control over your shutter speed, ISO, and white balance, this is the webcam you have been searching for.

## Detailed Breakdown

### 1. Uncompressed 1080p 60fps Video Quality: 9.5/10
Most webcams compress video before it travels through the USB port to your PC. This compression results in artifacting, slight visual lag, and color degradation.

* **Zero Latency, True 60fps:** The Elgato Facecam transmits **uncompressed YUV video** directly over a high-speed USB 3.0 connection. The result is a buttery-smooth, near-zero-latency feed. Whether you are waving your hands or dodging in a fast-paced game, the motion is incredibly life-like and free of stuttering.
* **Sony STARVIS Sensor:** Originally developed for security cameras to capture high-definition footage in pitch-black environments, the Sony STARVIS sensor has incredible light sensitivity. Under studio lighting, it produces stunningly sharp, broadcast-quality video. In low-light environments, it maintains color accuracy and controls digital noise far better than typical webcam sensors.

### 2. Custom Glass Optics & Fixed-Focus Precision: 9.3/10
* **All-Glass Prime Lens:** Elgato engineered a custom f/2.4 24mm equivalent glass lens to project light perfectly onto the Sony sensor, minimizing chromatic aberration and maximizing corner-to-corner sharpness.
* **Why Fixed Focus?** Standard autofocus webcams are notorious for "focus hunting." If you hold up your hands, lean forward, or shift in your chair, the camera's focus can wander, turning your face into a blurry mess for a few seconds. Elgato solved this by using a studio-grade **fixed-focus lens**. It is calibrated to keep everything from 30cm to 120cm (exactly where a person sits at a desk) in tack-sharp focus, eliminating focus drift completely.
* **The Caveat:** If you want to hold objects extremely close to the lens (under 30cm/12 inches), they will be blurry.

### 3. Camera Hub Software & Onboard Memory: 9.6/10
Elgato’s **Camera Hub** software is the absolute gold standard for webcam utility apps. It looks and behaves like the menu of a professional DSLR camera.

* **Complete DSLR-Like Control:** You can manually adjust ISO, shutter speed, exposure compensation, white balance, contrast, saturation, and digital zoom.
* **Hardware Saving:** The Facecam features built-in flash memory. Once you dial in your perfect camera settings on one PC, the settings are saved directly to the webcam’s internal hardware. If you plug it into another computer or stream console (like a PS5), your custom settings remain completely intact with no software running.

### 4. Hardware Philosophy: No Microphone, No Hassle: 9.0/10
* **The Anti-Microphone Stance:** The Elgato Facecam has **no built-in microphone**. While this might seem like a downside, Elgato knows their target audience uses dedicated USB mics (like the Elgato Wave:3) or XLR setups. By omitting a cheap, tinny internal microphone, they reduced hardware cost and complexity, reinvesting that budget entirely into the lens and sensor.
* **Industrial Build:** The Facecam is a chunky, square camera designed to dissipate heat efficiently. It features a robust mounting clamp that sits securely on monitors, and its detachable USB-C design makes it easy to replace cables or pack for travel.

## Verdict & Recommendation
The **Elgato Facecam** is a highly specialized, professional tool. It ignores the standard checklist of consumer features—there is no microphone, no autofocus, and no fancy multi-colored bodies—to focus entirely on delivering the absolute best broadcast-quality video. If you are a serious streamer, creator, or presenter who wants buttery-smooth 60fps motion, flawless clarity, and professional DSLR-like control over your image, the Elgato Facecam is an incredible investment.
