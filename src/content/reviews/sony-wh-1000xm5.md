The Sony WH-1000XM5 represents a significant shift in design for Sony's flagship noise-cancelling series. After years of iterative updates, the XM5 brings a fresh aesthetic and improved performance that aims to stay ahead of competitors like Bose and Apple.

## Sound Quality
Sony has introduced a new 30mm carbon fiber driver which, while smaller than the previous 40mm driver, offers more rigidity and less weight. The result is a more refined soundstage with tighter bass and exceptional clarity in the mid-high frequencies.

## Noise Cancellation
Equipped with two processors controlling eight microphones, the XM5 is remarkably good at silencing high-frequency noise like human voices and city traffic. In our tests, it consistently outperformed the XM4 and the Bose QC45 in varied environments.

## Verdict
If you want the best noise cancellation on the market today, the Sony WH-1000XM5 is it. While the price is steep and the non-folding design might irritate some, the improvements in voice calls and ANC performance make it a worthy investment for professionals.
