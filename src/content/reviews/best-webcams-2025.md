## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Streaming Webcam
When shopping for a webcam, don't just look at the megapixel count. Consider these crucial features:

### 1. Resolution and Frame Rate
* **1080p at 30fps:** The standard for business, school, and remote work. It is perfectly sharp and requires less network bandwidth.
* **1080p at 60fps:** Crucial for streamers and content creators. 60 frames per second captures smooth, lifelike motion, eliminating stutter.
* **2K (1440p) and 4K:** Offers extreme detail and allows you to crop/zoom in on your face digitally using software without losing clarity.

### 2. Autofocus vs. Fixed Focus
* **Autofocus:** Essential if you move around, hold up objects to the camera, or lean in and out of frame. The camera automatically adjusts to keep you sharp.
* **Fixed Focus (Budget):** Standard on very cheap webcams. Set at a generic distance, meaning objects too close or too far will be blurry.
* **Fixed Focus (Premium - e.g., Elgato):** Studio-grade fixed focus. Tuned to keep everything within a standard desk distance (30cm to 120cm) perfectly sharp, avoiding focus \"hunting\" artifacts.

### 3. Lighting and Exposure Correction
Webcams have tiny camera sensors. This means they are inherently bad in low-light environments.
Look for webcams with robust automatic light correction (like Logitech's RightLight or Anker's low-light algorithms). Having High Dynamic Range (HDR) support is also incredibly useful if you have a bright background (like a window) that would normally silhouette your face.

### 4. Field of View (FOV)
* **65° FOV:** A tight, narrow angle that focuses strictly on your head and shoulders. Great for hiding a messy room or busy office background.
* **78° - 82° FOV:** The standard mid-range view. Shows your head, shoulders, and a bit of your desk/room.
* **90°+ FOV:** A wide-angle view. Great for boardroom meetings, displaying a whiteboard, or showing a multi-person setup. Prioritize webcams with *adjustable* FOV via software.
