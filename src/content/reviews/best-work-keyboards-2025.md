## Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Mechanical Keyboard for Work

When choosing a mechanical keyboard for professional environments, prioritize comfort, acoustics, and seamless wireless connectivity.

### 1. Keyboard Layouts
75% Compact arranges the arrow keys and navigation keys directly adjacent to the main typing block, saving desk space while preserving the function row and arrows. This is the ideal layout for most modern office spaces.

### 2. Switch Types: Quiet vs. Tactile
* **Tactile (Brown):** Features a gentle physical "bump" at the actuation point. Excellent for touch typists as it confirms the keypress, while remaining relatively quiet. **Highly recommended for work.**
* **Linear (Red):** Smooth, consistent travel with no tactile bump. Extremely quiet, great for rapid typing.

### 3. Switch Height: Low-Profile vs. Full-Height
* **Full-Height:** Traditional mechanical feel with deep travel. Offers the most satisfying "bounce."
* **Low-Profile:** Slimmer build with shorter travel. Extremely ergonomic and easy on the wrists.
